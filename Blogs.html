THEORIES AND LAWS OF PHYSICS:

Aether Theory:

Aether theories (also known as ether theories) propose the existence of a medium, a space-filling substance, or a field as a transmission medium for the propagation of electromagnetic or gravitational forces. More about Aether Theory : Aether Theory

Multiverse theory:

A multiverse is a hypothetical group of multiple universes. Together, these universes comprise everything that exists: the entirety of space, time, energy, matter, information, and the physics and constants that describe them. More about Multiverse theory : Multiverse theory

Holographic principle:

The holographic principle is an axiom in string theories and a supposed property of quantum gravity that states that the description of a volume of space can be thought of as encoded on a lower-dimensional boundary to the region — such as a light-like boundary like a gravitational horizon. More about Holographic principle : Holographic principle

Hawking Radiation:

Hawking radiation is theoretical black body radiation that is theorized to be released outside a black hole’s event horizon because of relativistic quantum effects. It is named after the physicist Stephen Hawking, who developed a theoretical argument for its existence in 1974. More about Hawking Radiation : Hawking Radiation

F-Theory:

In theoritical physics, F-theory is a branch of string theory developed by Iranian physicist Cumrun Vafa. The new vacua described by F-theory were discovered by Vafa and allowed string theorists to construct new realistic vacua — in the form of F-theory. The letter "F" supposedly stands for "Father". More about F-theory : F-Theory

Dilation:

Dilation is one of the basic operations in mathematical morphology. Originally developed for binary images, it has been expanded first to grayscale images, and then to complete lattices. The dilation operation usually uses a structuring element for probing and expanding the shapes contained in the input image. More about Dilation : Dilation

Spin-Statics Theorem:

In quantum mechanics, the spin-statics theorem relates the intrinsic spin of a particle (angular momentum not due to the orbital motion) to the particle statistics it obeys. In units of the reduced plank’s constant ħ, all particles that move in 3 dimensions have either integer spin or half-integer spin. More about spin-statics theorem : Spin-Statics Theorem

Perturbation theorem:

Perturbation theory is a method for continuously improving a previously obtained approximate solution to a problem, and it is an important and general method for finding approximate solutions to the Schrödinger equation. More about Perturbation theorem: Perturbation theorem

Bell's Theorem:

More about Bell's theorem : Bell's theorem shows that no theory that satisfies the conditions imposed can reproduce the probabilistic predictions of quantum mechanics under all circumstances. The principal condition used to derive Bell inequalities is a condition that may be called Bell locality, or factorizability. More about Bell's theorem : Bell's Theorem

Beer-Lambert Theorem:

The Beer-Lambert law states that: for a given material sample path length and concentration of the sample are directly proportional to the absorbance of the light. More about Beer-Lambert theorem : Beer-Lambert Theorem

Avogadro's law:

Avogadro's law, a statement that under the same conditions temperature and pressure, equal volumes of different gases contain an equal number of molecules. More about Avogadro's law : Avogadro's law
